# Phase 3 — Desk briefs (2031)

## PM

The Second Gate tested everything built since 2027; the country lived whatever your earlier choices made of those eleven days. The megaproject's realized savings are now a published number with claimants, the election is in the spring, and abroad the great powers discuss counting accelerators like warheads while the first ward robots ship to allied hospitals.

**The decision.** Round three is reactionary: the savings are published, the claimants are in the corridor, and half your options exist only because of the numbers the table made in 2027–29. Pay the bridge if the framework can still carry one. Consolidate if you can bear the message it sends. The locked cards on your desk are locked by arithmetic, not by law.

## AKAVA

This is the table everything since 2027 pointed at. Your signature is the only legitimacy the dividend can have. The first care robots abroad mean the machine is coming for your members' hands next, not just their desks; whatever rights you win now are the template for that negotiation too.

**The decision.** Everything now depends on what was banked earlier: the strike exists only if the ballot was never spent, backdating is only owed where sharing failed. Sign with teeth, grind through a work-to-rule winter, or save your own and let the unorganized pay. Read the locks before the rhetoric.

## TI

The automation levy is on the table. Your members can live with a number; they cannot live with open-ended.

**The decision.** The levy is on the table and your room to move was priced by the decade: a confident sector can buy the peace privately, a cornered one reaches for the capital strike, and both bills say who pays on the label. A number with a cap is still the cheapest sentence in this room.

## COUNTY

Whatever is funded, you operate: retraining desks, care reinvestment, consolidation targets, or the unfunded aftermath of a punt. The first care-robot pilots abroad put an asterisk on your ten-year staffing plan that nobody can price yet.

**The decision.** The dividend has claimants and the wards have receipts. Partner with the bridge, bank against winter, or take the moves that only desperation or excellence unlock: claw the money to care off the top, triage the thinnest wards, or sell the model abroad while the buyers poach your nurses.

## AALTO

If the dividend passes, somebody must prove the retraining works, annually and in public. If consolidation wins, the analytics contracts are lucrative and your name goes on the cuts.

**The decision.** The manifesto named the broken promise; the dividend could repair it. Certify the guarantee, repair Eetu's cohort specifically, or make the radical universal offer — each needs a framework that can pay. The other direction is always open: analytics for the cuts, talent from abroad, and the promise lapses with a footnote.

## STARTUP

Your biggest customer is about to become reliable, fast, or frozen.

**The decision.** The exit window is open exactly as wide as the country made it. Sell at the top, pledge your way out of the levy, or open-source the stack and buy the ecosystem's memory of you. Every card on this table reprices somebody else's.

## HVK

The Second Gate proved whatever it proved. The window to make its lessons permanent closes when the campaign starts.

**The decision.** The Gate graded the decade and your menu is the report card: drills can be normalized if the clock is real, audits can be securitized only if the crisis is remembered, and the doctrine can go public only if your hand is strong. The tempting card is always stand-down; the next gate prices it.
