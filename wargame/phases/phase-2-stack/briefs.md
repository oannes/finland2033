# Phase 2 — Desk briefs (2029)

## PM

The benchmark is public: American and Chinese models lead the European tier by twenty months, and the lead grows, because the leading labs now use their models to improve their models. Ordinary machine work has become cheap for everyone; only the frontier stays scarce and priced. The megaproject must choose its engine this procurement cycle, and retrofitting later costs triple.

**The decision.** Declare the public core sovereign, and Finland runs on machines it can inspect, eighteen months behind the frontier, with the gap on your record. Sign the Washington agreement, and Finland gets the best models under conditions written elsewhere. Let each agency choose, and you avoid the fight and inherit three incompatible systems. Half your coalition feels betrayed whichever way you turn.

## COUNTY

Frontier models are measurably better medicine this year: fewer missed diagnoses, faster triage. European models are defensible data and a stack under Finnish law. You are the only actor whose trade-off is counted in patient outcomes, and both mistakes bury quietly.

**The decision.** Migrate county services to the European tier and you accept weaker tools now for control that lasts. Buy the frontier lane and patients benefit this year on machines that answer elsewhere. Run both and your staff maintains two systems with one workforce. Whatever Helsinki declares, your procurement is the country's real stack.

## STARTUP

You need frontier capability to compete abroad, and you hold the most sensitive dataset in the country. Genome data, once transferred, cannot be untransferred. The sector reads your stack choice as its forecast.

**The decision.** Build on the European tier and your moat stays under EU law while your models trail the frontier. Take the attested lane and you compete at full strength under someone else's terms of service. Split the company and you pay for both, honestly and expensively. You are the proof or the counter-example for whatever the state declares.

## SAK

Every guarantee written into the megaproject is enforceable only against a counterparty European courts can reach. An American annex cannot be grieved. You are pro-sovereignty for the least ideological reason at the table: jurisdiction.

**The decision.** Say so publicly and you hand the sovereignty camp labour's weight. Accept the lane if it keeps jobs growing, and your members work on machines their contracts cannot reach. Stay silent and the stack is decided without labour in the room.

## TI

Your exporters need the attested lane to serve their customers. Your energy and construction members want the sovereign build's order book. Both are right, and one press conference will set the association's position.

**The decision.** Underwrite the sovereign build and industry's capital anchors the European tier. Lobby for the lane and your exporters keep their access while the build loses its bank. Go public with the split and you keep both camps and lose the voice.

## AALTO

The claim "the European tier is good enough for 80% of public tasks" is a slogan until somebody tests it. You are the only actor who can run that test. The same faculty hours can instead go to joint labs on the frontier lane, where the best tools and the best students already point.

**The decision.** Certify the European tier and a political migration becomes an engineering claim, whatever the result. Follow the capability and your graduates work at the frontier, elsewhere. Measure both and endorse neither, and both camps quote you against the other.

## HVK

Doctrine says critical functions belong on infrastructure Finland can inspect and, if needed, run alone. Your own services want the best models available, which are not European. Both statements are true, and you will be quoted either way.

**The decision.** Argue doctrine and you defend resilience at the price of capability your colleagues want. Argue conditional access, with exit drills and escrowed weights, and you make dependence survivable instead of pretending it away. Map the dependencies without a recommendation, and the map is excellent and late.
