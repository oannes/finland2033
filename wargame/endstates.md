# Endstates — composing the 2033 debrief screen

The endstate is **composed, not selected**: STACK (from Phase 2) × Phase 3 outcome class, modulated by SECURE_ARCH and the election result. The software renders: archetype text + final data block + Marja's and Eetu's endings + the election line + the debrief question, and the ghost chart of everything not chosen.

## Archetype texts

### E1. The Finnish Model (euro × dividend)
The full construction: an inspectable engine, a negotiated deployment, a funded transition. Finland never closed the capability gap; it made the gap survivable, priced it honestly, and sold the method: by 2035 eleven countries have bought the compact framework, the certification regime, or both. The standing cost is the gap itself, booked annually and argued about honestly. *Debrief: legitimacy turned out to be a production factor; what did it cost to learn that, and could the 2027 table have bought it cheaper?*

### E2. Sovereign and Stalled (euro × punt)
The stack is owned and underused: sovereign capacity serving a public sector whose money-question was never answered. Sovereignty achieved as infrastructure, refused as politics. Queues rule; the gap widens from both ends. *Debrief: sovereignty without a settlement is a very expensive museum.*

### E3. Efficient Fortress (euro × balance sheet)
The rare bird: consolidation executed on an inspectable stack. More resilient than E6, more solvent than E1, colder than both; the machine is Finland's own, and so is the loneliness of the people it processed. *Debrief: the stack was never the problem.*

### E4. Dividend on a Leash (us × dividend)
The social peace is real; the substrate is rented. The settlement works, osaamistakuu funded, care reinvested, until the lane's terms move, which is not a Finnish decision. If the 2028 tender bought its exit clauses, that risk is a drill; if it didn't, it is a rerun of the Second Gate with better paperwork. *Debrief: who countersigns a guarantee?*

### E5. Tenant's Rest (us × punt)
The lane provides, the state declines to decide, the queues absorb. Finland as a comfortable subscriber: private economy competitive, public money unallocated, the gap between them the widest in the OECD. *Debrief: the machine's proceeds are still in the account. So is the question.*

### E6. The Algorithmic North (us × balance sheet)
The FT profile, structurally complete: surplus, throughput, series-low trust, landlord risk. Every index green but one, and the 2034 lane repricing meets a state whose civilian fallback muscle was never funded (unless the 2028 exit clauses were bought; then render the one working drill as the epilogue's grace note). *Debrief: name the one index aloud.*

### E7. Patchwork Dividend (split × dividend)
The settlement administers coherence onto an incoherent substrate; heroic institutional embroidery. Slowest of the dividend endstates, most instructive: consent turned out to be buildable even where architecture wasn't. *Debrief: the settlement fixed what the stack decision broke; imagine both.*

### E8. The Long Fudge (split × punt)
Nothing decided in 2028, 2029 or 2031. Finland 2033 is Finland 2025 with older systems, longer queues, lower trust and a Politico nickname. The game's null hypothesis, fully rendered, and the prologue's decade, restarted. *Debrief: name the single earliest move that would have forked this timeline.* (The software shows the ghost chart; all the grey nodes not taken.)

### E9. Split-State Efficiency (split × balance sheet)
Consolidation executed on three stacks under two legal regimes: the cuts arrive at 60% efficiency with 100% of the legitimacy bill. The worst ratio in the matrix. *Debrief: sequencing; the same decisions in a different order were three other Finlands.*

## The lesson (facilitator's closing)

**Official:** Legitimacy is a production factor. Productivity without consent stalls (2027); capability without resilience is rented, not owned (2029); gains without distribution get politically repossessed (2031). A welfare state can be automated exactly as fast as it is renegotiated; no faster. Done early, the tripartite reflex is not the obstacle to the productivity leap; it is the productivity technology.

**Emotional carrier:** the Marja–Eetu theorem. A system that protects insiders while stalling its reformers fails twice; once slowly (Marja leaves), once generationally (Eetu never enters). Only paths that bring both inside before the external shock arrive at a 2033 worth having. Their endings on this screen are the proof, either way.

**Debrief mechanic:** the ghost chart. Same decisions, different order; three other Finlands. Ask the room which grey node they would trade for, and what it would have cost the person who had to choose it.

## Epilogue cards (control team, optional, post-scoring)
- **The lane reprices (us-stack archetypes):** 72-hour degradation, 2034. Renders per SECURE_ARCH.
- **The tier catches up (euro-stack archetypes):** the European tier reaches frontier-minus-6-months in narrow domains, 2035; the gap was a decade, not a destiny.
- **The robots arrive (all):** the first care-robot cohort ships to Finnish wards in 2034; cheaper each quarter, like the minds before them. Who writes their schedules, and whether the care they free up becomes time or savings, depends on the archetype.
- **The next cohort (all):** Eetu's cousin graduates in 2034. One sentence on what she walks into, per archetype. End on her.
