## Baseline (2026)
Maria Lehtola, 40, Helsinki. In 2021, after studying service design alongside
her work at Kela, she left for a private consultancy: she knew how to improve
clients' lives, but had grown frustrated by her inability to change how the
agency itself operated. Five years later she returned to Kela's strategy
department for a second attempt. She likes red wine, good Netflix series and
institutions that can still learn. Whether Kela can is decided in these rooms.

## Goals and aspirations
To prove the institution can learn: ship the staged plan she brought back, keep a reform unit alive past one budget cycle, and be vindicated rather than expelled a second time.

## What she measures
Reform capacity from within: does the state absorb or expel the people who come
back to fix it? Her career tracks whether public AI is deployed by design or by
deadline, and whether a returner's plan can survive contact with the machine
that rejected it once already.
