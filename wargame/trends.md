# Trend cards (the weather — not decidable)

Eight cards shown at briefing and re-shown at every phase. Players may not argue with these; the control team enforces them. Each card: the global trend, its Finnish landing point, and the number to remember.

## TR-1 Capability keeps compounding
The machines improve faster each year than the year before, because the labs have begun pointing them at their own research. Nobody — markets, ministries, militaries — is pricing how much further this can run; the honest range spans everything from "a strong decade" to "a different civilization". Every Finnish plan built on the recent past's regularities is a plan built on the wrong decade. Not decidable; only survivable.

## TR-2 Compute is capital, and capital cycles
Compute is scarce and contested now, and will commoditize later — in that order, and the order is the trap. Buildouts boom and bust; whoever holds capacity through a bust names their price in the recovery. For a small country the question is never "can we out-build them" but "what do we hold when the wheel turns".

## TR-3 Finland adopts faster than it governs
37.8% of Finnish firms use AI (EU #2); 66% use genAI (EU #1, EIB); MoF requires human verification of all AI administrative output (2/2025). **Finnish landing:** the adoption lead is an exposure lead — dependence deepens faster than oversight matures. **Number: 66%.**

## TR-4 The workforce shrinks on schedule
Working-age population −57,000 by 2030; dependency ratio passes 70/100; care, ECEC and software chronically short. **Finnish landing:** every service promise requires migration, automation or rationing — all three politically priced. **Number: 70/100.**

## TR-5 The fiscal vice tightens
Debt →~91% of GDP, deficit ~4.5%, defence spend →5% of GDP by 2032. **Finnish landing:** AI productivity in Kela, counties and municipalities stops being an opportunity and becomes a line item in the spending framework. **Number: 5%.**

## TR-6 Entry-level rungs erode first
US data: −16% relative employment for 22–25-year-olds in AI-exposed occupations; entry software/customer service −20% (Stanford/ADP). **Finnish landing:** each graduating cohort finds fewer first jobs; frustration accumulates in the AI-fluent generation. **Number: −16%.**

## TR-7 Infrastructure lives under threat
≥11 Baltic cables/pipelines damaged in two years (Estlink 2, Dec 2024); eastern border closed; Baltic Sentry patrols. **Finnish landing:** every datacenter, cable and grid link carries a threat model and an insurance premium — and security competence becomes a Finnish export. **Number: 11.**

## TR-8 Access is becoming a licence
June 2026: US executive order creates pre-release security review of frontier models; Commerce orders suspension of foreign access to two models; EU Cloud & AI Act sets four sovereignty assurance levels. **Finnish landing:** the models Finland runs on are becoming licensed goods, revocable at someone else's crisis. **Number: 2 models, 19 days (in-game).**

## TR-9 The robots are late, and then sudden
Machine minds arrived years before machine hands. The wards, warehouses and home-care rounds that carry Finland's labour shortage are still human — but the same research engines now grinding through language are being pointed at the physical world, and when that breaks, it breaks like 2022 did: a demo, then a product, then a procurement question, inside two years. Care work is the last labour buffer and the first robotics market.

## TR-10 Thinking gets cheap; the edge stays dear
The price of ordinary machine cognition collapses every year — what cost a salary in 2024 costs a subscription in 2027 and a rounding error by 2031. But the frontier premium does not collapse with it: the best models stay scarce, priced, and politically allocated. Every organisation on Earth is learning to live on the cheap tier and to beg, buy or build access to the dear one. So is every country.
