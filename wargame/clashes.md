# Collisions

Seven standing confrontations run through the table, two through every seat. They
fire in every phase, after the decisions are locked, by one rule the players are
told in plain words:

**Force overruns negotiation. Negotiation binds those who wait. Waiting outlasts force.**

(Mechanically: each action's tag is its stance. A = force, B = negotiate, H = wait.
In each confrontation below, compare the two stances: A beats B, B beats H, H beats A.
Same stance = stand-off, nothing fires. The loser pays the listed price, in the
currency of their own goals. Balanced by construction: every actor sits in exactly
two confrontations, and every actor's loss rate depends only on reading the table,
not on which seat they drew.)

Authoring format: `- if ACTOR loses: EFFECTS | narrative line`. Effects use the
same grammar as action lines: `POLL -3`, `RES -0.3`, `data trust -1.5`.
